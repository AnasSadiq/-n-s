History: The Paralympic Games were first held in 1960 in Rome, Italy. They were initially known as the Stoke Mandeville Games, named after the British village where they originated.

Participants: Athletes with various disabilities participate in the Paralympics, including physical disabilities, amputations, visual impairments, and intellectual disabilities.

Sports: The Paralympic Games feature a wide range of sports adapted for athletes with disabilities. These sports are categorized into different impairment groups, such as wheelchair sports, amputee sports, visually impaired sports, and others. Examples of sports include wheelchair basketball, para swimming, para athletics (track and field), para cycling, para powerlifting, and many more.

Events: Like the Olympics, the Paralympics have both individual and team events across various sports. Athletes compete for medals in their respective events, showcasing their skills and abilities.
